Counter-Strike

Release year: 2000

Publisher: Valve Corporation

Genre: first person, multiplayer

Rate(Steam): 10/10

"Counter-Strike" is a pioneering first-person shooter game that began as a mod for "Half-Life" and quickly evolved into a standalone title, developed by Valve. First released in 1999, it set the standard for what competitive shooters could be, with its fast-paced, skill-based gameplay. The game pits two teams against each other: terrorists and counter-terrorists, with objectives that vary by mode—ranging from bomb planting and defusal to hostage rescue. Over the years, "Counter-Strike" has been lauded for its simplicity, depth, and highly competitive nature, which made it a staple in esports tournaments around the world. Its success spawned several updates and sequels, including "Counter-Strike: Source" and "Counter-Strike: Global Offensive," each enhancing the franchise with improved graphics, new mechanics, and more intricate maps, maintaining the series’ position as a cornerstone in the genre of tactical shooters.
